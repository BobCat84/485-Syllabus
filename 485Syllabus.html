Course Description

This course provides an experiential experience in innovation and entrepreneurship. It will teach you specific methods to assess and understand the industry, customers, and competitors for a new venture. You will then learn how to translate those insights into a winning venture idea, a business model, and set of distinctive new products and services.

This course is useful for:

  • Future Entrepreneurs: Students who wish to start their own venture, today or at some point in the future
  • Corporate Innovators: Students who work or will be working for a corporation and want to create entirely new businesses within that corporation
  • Social Impact Leaders: Those interested in non-profit or NGO's, learning to think like a customer-centric organization

Course Focus Areas

Part 1 - Ideation:

  • Industry and/or stakeholder analysis and venture opportunity assessment
  • Creating the venture concept based on identifying customer needs
  • Designing an attractive and sustainable business model
  • Develop a fully featured product and service strategy
  • Positioning products and services against competitors already in the market

Part 2 - Implementation:

  • Identify the right types of investors for your venture
  • Create realistic, achievable financial projections
  • Complete a concise, powerful venture questionnaire
  • Develop and film a winning pitch that captures the hearts and minds of investors and stakeholders

Learning Outcomes

  • Expand knowledge: Gain perspective on basic aspects of innovation, ideation, entrepreneurship, and successful business models
  • Apply to a specific innovative idea: Apply fundamental principles of innovation and integrate with interdisciplinary coursework to your team's innovative idea
  • Analyze & think critically: Integrate and apply models of innovation, strategy, problem solving and critical thinking
  • Synthesize the business idea: Work on teams to compile a comprehensive business questionnaire and short film pitch
  • Seek global connections: Examine innovation and entrepreneurship under a global umbrella, looking for opportunities anywhere

Emergency Procedures

Classroom Evacuation Procedures

When fire sirens sound in UA buildings, Arizona law and University policy require you to evacuate.

  • Leave the building immediately in an orderly manner using established evacuation routes and stairs
  • Take immediately available personal items (purses, backpacks, keys, phones)
  • Do not use elevators
  • People with handicaps should remain in the nearest stairwell until help arrives
  • Help others identify safe passages
  • Move away from the building - at least 200 feet away
  • For McClelland Hall: go to or beyond the Speedway underpass or across Helen Street
  • Follow all orders from authorized personnel
  • Stay together as a class if possible
  • Do not re-enter the building until directed by emergency response personnel
